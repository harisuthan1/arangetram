Dance Repertoire

❁ ❁ ❁

Pushpanjali

Pushpanjali is the opening invocation of a Bharatanatyam performance and means "offering of flowers." Here the dancer offers her humble salutations to the divine, the teacher, and the audience. Through rhythmic footwork and expressive movements, she seeks blessings to commence the performance with devotion.

Ragam: JogTalam: AdiComposer: Madurai R. Muralidharan

Jaya Jaya Swamin

Salutations to Lord Ganesha, the elephant-faced one with gracefully curved tusks, He removes obstacles of His devotees. Revered by Indra and the Devas, He's adorned with divine splendor. He dispels darkness and shows compassion to the composer.

Ragam: NattaTalam: AdiComposer: Narayana Theerthar

Jathiswaram

Jathiswaram is a vibrant nritta composition that blends rhythmic patterns and melodic swaras. It emphasizes the dancer's agility, precision, and graceful geometry, bringing music to life through movement.

Ragam: SaraswatiTalam: RupakaComposer: Kancheepuram Ellappa Mudaliar

Varnam

Varnam is the centerpiece of a Bharatanatyam recital. It is a richly layered, expressive composition that explores the dancer's deep emotional dialogue with the deity. The Nayika pleads with Lord Muruga, the peacock-rider, to appear before her. Born from Lord Shiva's third eye and the vanquisher of Surapadman, He stands with his consorts Valli and Devasena. The radiant son of Parvati, He delights in the devotion of His followers and is revered even as the teacher of the sacred Pranava. Unable to find Him, she seeks His grace.

Ragam: LathangiTalam: AdiComposer: Andavan Pichai

✦ Intermission ✦

Chinnanchiru Kiliye

A mother's greatest joy is her little Kannamma, her precious treasure and shining light. Her laughter and dance fills her heart with pride and happiness. A mother's love lives forever in the joy, grace, and light of her little child.

Ragam: RagamalikaTalam: RupakaComposer: Mahakavi Bharatiyar

Meenakshi Thaye

O Meenakshi, radiant Goddess of Madurai, daughter of Malayadwaja and beloved of Lord Sundareshwara, she is the embodiment of grace, strength, and divine beauty. With fish-like eyes and a moon-like face, she vanquished Mahishasura and blessed the world with music, devotion, and compassion.

Ragam: AbhogiTalam: AdiComposer: K. N. Dandayudapani Pillai

Ananda Natamidum Padan

In the Golden Hall of Chidambaram, Lord Nataraja performs his cosmic dance of bliss, surrounded by divine music and worshipped by sages and Devas. With Goddess Shivakami beside him, he sustains the universe through his eternal dance.

Ragam: KedaragowlaTalam: AdiComposer: Papanasam Sivan

Kuzhaloothi Manamellam

As Lord Krishna plays his enchanting flute, the world fills with joy, beauty, and divine bliss. Nature dances in harmony around him, while his music melts every heart and washes away all sorrows.

Ragam: KambhojiTalam: AdiComposer: Oothukkadu Venkata Kavi

Thillana

A Thillana is a vibrant and rhythmic composition that forms the grand finale of a Bharatanatyam recital. It is marked by fast-paced movements, intricate footwork, and expressive energy, showcasing the dancer's technical brilliance and grace. This Thillana is in praise of Goddess Bhuvaneshwari, the Cosmic Mother who gives auspiciousness and happiness to her devotees.

Ragam: RevathiTalam: AdiComposer: Madurai N. Krishnan

Bharatanatyam & Arangetram

❁ ❁ ❁

Bharatanatyam

Bharatanatyam is one of India's ancient classical dance forms, originating in the temples of South India. Traditionally performed as a devotional offering, it is rooted in the ancient text Bharata's Natyashastra, dating back to around 200 B.C.

Arangetram

Arangetram, a Tamil word meaning "ascending the stage," marks a pivotal moment in a Bharatanatyam dancer's journey: the first full-length solo performance. This debut celebrates years of rigorous training, discipline, and devotion. More than a performance, it symbolizes both mastery and a new beginning in the dancer's artistic path.

Kavyamridula Venkatesan

❁ ❁ ❁

Kavyamridula Venkatesan is 18 years old and a recent graduate of Monta Vista High School. She will be attending the University of California, San Diego in the fall as a freshman.

Kavyamridula has been learning Bharatanatyam for nearly 12 years, including the past 8 years under the tutelage of Guru Smt. Megha Shetty at BayArea Nritya Gurukul. Throughout her training, she has participated in numerous traditional dramas and events and competitions, deepening her appreciation for Indian culture and heritage.

Her love for Indian dance extends beyond Bharatanatyam. She served as captain of her high school's Bhangra team for the past three years, further nurturing her passion for cultural expression. As she begins her college journey, Kavyamridula hopes to continue staying connected to the dance community and sharing her love for Indian arts.

Smt. Megha A. Shetty

❁ ❁ ❁

Smt. Megha A. Shetty, founder and artistic director of BayArea Nritya Gurukul, is dedicated to preserving and teaching the rich tradition of Bharatanatyam. Trained in the Thanjavur style, she began her journey at the renowned Sri Rajarajeswari Bharatanatya Kalamandir in Mumbai. Megha had the privilege of training under several renowned Gurus, including the late Kalaimamani Guru T.K. Mahalingam Pillai, Guru Vasanth Kumar, Guru Vishwanath, Smt. Shanthi Shekhar, and Guru Lata Raman.

She further deepened her knowledge by completing a Master's degree in Bharatanatyam from Aria University, studying under eminent scholars such as Dr. Karuna Vijayendra, Dr. Sujatha Mohan, Dr. Padmaja Suresh, and Dr. S. Divyasena. Megha gratefully acknowledges the unwavering support of her Gurus and parents, who have guided her artistic journey. She is currently pursuing Nattuvangam lessons under Guru Sri. Mayuram Shankar.

Through BayArea Nritya Gurukul, she inspires students to pursue excellence, discipline, and passion in the sacred art of Bharatanatyam.

BayArea Nritya Gurukul

With the blessings of her Gurus, Smt. Megha A. Shetty founded BayArea Nritya Gurukul, a vibrant center for learning and preserving Bharatanatyam. Under her guidance, students follow a structured syllabus and appear for Akhil Bharatiya Gandharva Mahavidyalaya exams. They actively perform at cultural events earning accolades and recognition for their talent and commitment. The school's acclaimed annual productions celebrate Indian heritage while addressing relevant social themes through classical dance.

The Orchestra

❁ ❁ ❁

Smt. Jayanthi Umesh Vocal

Smt. Jayanthi Umesh started her initial music training with Vidushi M. Janaki and later under the tutelage of the well-known exponent of classical Indian music Vidushi Sugandha Raman. She passed the Vidwath Examination of Karnataka State with honors and has advanced training from Shrimathi Asha Ramesh, Suguna Varadachari, and created music workshops conducted by Dr. M. Balamuralikrishna and Vidwan R. K. Srikantan. Some of her accomplishments and honors include: Best Musician award from Bangalore Gaayana Samaaja, invited performance at the Annamacharya festival at Tirupathi, performances at Cleveland Thyagaraja Aradhana, sponsored events of South India Fine Arts, and at various venues in Bangalore and USA. She founded Mahatee Music Academy in the San Francisco Bay Area to impart rigorous, collaborative and holistic Carnatic music education.

Sri. Srinath Bala Mridangam

Sri. Srinath Bala is a versatile percussionist with varied styles blended rhythmically into one. Having been initiated by Late Sri. Krishnan (Palghat Sri Mani Iyer style), he received intensive guidance under Sri. R. Ramesh (Karaikudi Mani Style) in Chennai, and later, under Maestro Umayalpuram K. Sivaraman. To his credit, he has uniquely combined the kannakku and naadam aspects from various gurus. He has accompanied top senior artists performing in India, Canada, Zambia, Singapore and the US. He has also performed in Jugalbandis and TalaVadya Concerts to showcase the solo percussion aspects. He has several recordings and has given lecture-demonstrations on 'LAYAM'. In 2006, he presented a TalaVadya concert titled "Music Through Membranes". Being a recipient of the PG Arts award, MSAC Artist Awards, and titles 'Kala Shreshtha' and 'Paramparaa Rakshak', he has over 30 years of concert experiences and is an extremely well sought after artist for music and dance concerts. He has been teaching students for many years and has successfully trained many students, who currently play Mridangam, Ghatam, and Kanjira actively in various music circles.

Smt. Sandhya Srinath Violin

Sandhya is an innately talented artist who showcases her true artistry in every bow! A gold medalist in her master's program in music and an early A-grade awardee for the All-India Radio, Sandhya has firmly established the school of Late Shri V. Janakiraman of AIR, Delhi. She learnt, and skillfully absorbed the styles of maestros, late Shri. Lalgudi G. Jayaraman and Prof. T.N. Krishnan within her. Along with top solo performances worldwide, she has also accompanied many senior musicians and dancers for 40 years. Some of her coveted awards include the Best Young Violinist, Music Academy, Chennai, India; and the Individual Artist Award from both PG Arts Council and the Maryland State Arts Council. Sandhya has also received titles such as Naada Brahma Kala Bhooshanam, Naada Brahma Kalai Mani, Vaadhya Kala Bhaarathi, Kala Shreshtha and Paramparaa Rakshak. She has numerous recordings to her credit and her versatility has led her to take on instrumental ensembles, jugalbandis with Hindustani artists, and fusion concerts with western musicians. Known for her sensitive renditions and scintillating artistry, she is a very highly sought after artist. She also actively teaches Violin to students all over the US.

Sri. Pasumarthi Venkataramana Flute

Pasumarthi Venkataramana is a senior flautist, with a performing experience of more than 4 decades. Introduced to flute played by his father, late Shri Viswanatha Rao and later trained in aesthetics by Dr Prapancham Sitaram, he has performed in India as well as many countries including USA, UK, Italy, Holland, Indonesia, and Australia. He has worked with eminent composers and dance exponents viz Lalgudi Jayaraman, Vyjayantimala Bali, Adyar Lakshman, Urmila Satyanarayan, Jayanthi Subramaniam, and many more. His flute is an added asset to performers. He has been teaching students as well in the divine art of flute playing.
